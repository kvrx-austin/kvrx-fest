KVRX FEST

May 10-11th
Austin, TX

LINEUP

* Austin artist

Frankie Cosmos

New York, NY

Greta Kline is the singer and songwriter behind the stage name, Frankie Cosmos. She began music career at age 19 with the release of her first album, Zentropy. Her indie pop sound resonates through all three of her albums; continually honing her craft from one to the next. Her lyrics are well thought out and provide a sneaky melancholy sense of humor. She is also the former bass guitarist of the band Porches. Some songs to check out: "Fool", "Jesse" (2018), and "Young".

Palm

Philedelphia, PA

Full of interesting rhythms and unique sounds, adding Palm to our lineup was a no brainer. This exciting group can get a crowd pining for more. Get it?

Drab Majesty

Los Angeles, CA

Drab Majesty brings the best of post-punk and new wave together. With reverb-laden guitars and brooding atmosphere, the band blends their '80s influences with darkwave and cold synthesizers, especially in their 2017 release "The Demonstration". The group commits to well-informed theatrically, allowing for Drab Majesty to exist as both a listening experience and a state of being.

Fat Tony

Houston, TX

"Houston native Anthony Lawson Obi, better known by his stage name Fat Tony, is set to take the hip-hop scene by storm. While Fat Tony derives inspiration from a variety of sources, it's easy to hear how his Houston upbringing has affected his eclectic and refreshing music.
Following the release of his 2018 album 10,000 Hours, Fat Tony continues to perfect his art and expand his sound."

Abhi the Nomad

Austin, TX

Austin rapper Abhi the Nomad's stage name is inspired by a childhood spent all over the globe with his father, an Indian diplomat. He seems to have inherited his father's knack for negotiation and applied it to his own craft; his full-length debut, 2018's Marbled, strikes a pitch-perfect balance between rap, r&b, indie rock, and more. Abhi's latest single, "FLUSH", signals an even more left-field turn for the rapper, so we can’t wait to see what he brings to his Fest set!

Omni

Atlanta, GA

Omni has mastered their own brand of high-energy post-punk music that always seems to go in unexpected and exciting directions. Last year, Omni played one of KVRX's unofficial SXSW showcases, and all the DJs at KVRX are excited for new music. We truly can't wait for the 3 piece band to bring their talent to Austin for KVRX Fest.

Video Age

New Orleans, LA

Video Age provides an echoey synth-pop that just keeps on giving. This New Orleans based group uses vintage synthesizers, classic guitars, and many more instrumentals to deliver groovy hooks. The duet delivers well thought out vocals complemented with tasteful instrumentation. Pure fantastic sounding retro indie pop. Be sure to check out “Lover Surreal” and “Dance Square”.

Sexy Dex and the Fresh

New Orleans, LA

Sexy Dex and the Fresh is a synthpop band that incorporates electronic and funk sensibilities into their songs. If you take a look at their latest EP, Don't Play My B Sides, you can infer what kind of music these folks are putting out. Let me give you some advice whenever you listen to them: get up & shake your tailfeathers- it'll be hard to resist.

Mamalarky

Los Angeles, LA

Photo by Julia Arden Dixon

KVRX can’t wait to welcome station alumni Mamalarky back to where it all began. Based in Los Angeles but born in Austin, the self-proclaimed “genre-fluid rainbow rock artists” effortlessly combine elements of psych, rock, and pop. Their self-released debut EP Fundamental Thrive Hive announced the three-piece as one of the freshest artists to emerge from the city in quite some time.

Why Bonnie

Austin, TX

Why Bonnie is a unique quintet who originate from Austin, TX. They have defined themselves as “bedroom pop that moved to the couch”. Why Bonnie has a very genuine sound that engages the listener and guides them through various emotions. Give their 2018 album "Nightgown" a listen.

TC Superstar

Austin, TX

Over the past two years, TC Superstar has become a staple among the Austin area. Consisting of former UT students, the synthwave group is known for their energetic live shows, complete with dancers and choreography. Their pop-oriented tunes give us all the chance to be Toyota Corolla superstars, if only for one night.

Blushing

Austin, TX

Austin, Texas’ Blushing is a favorite here at KVRX. Composed of two married duos, it’s no surprise that having a strong, cohesive sound is one of Blushing’s strong suits. They continuously impress us with their sweet, shoegaze sounds.

Xetas

Austin, TX

Austin's own XETAS are known throughout the city for their bombastic and fast noise-punk that might just leave your ears ringing. With a new album expected to be out at some point this year, we can't wait to see what they have in store for KVRX Fest.

Caroline Says

Austin, TX

Alabama to Texas transplant Caroline Sallee churns out wistful, folk-y pop music enveloped in Southern nostalgia. Her most recent album, No Fool Like an Old Fool came out on Western Vinyl in 2018.

Smiile

Austin, TX

SMiiLE is described as an "experimental pop band... born out of a shooting star." This description is apt as SMiiLE crafts infectious pop songs that can be played on a beach or in a club. We'd recommend their latest album Mystical Experience / Someone Who Can Do Both to get prepped to nod your head at KVRX Fest.

Dezorah

McAllen, TX

Dezorah brings a truly unique and invigorating musical experience to the stage and studio alike. Their refreshing post-prog sensibilities paired with haunting, unforgettable, and bilingual vocals by UT Austin alum Danica Salizar will leave you speechless every time. Watch their jaw-dropping Local Live performance at the KVRX studios from June 2018.

Mike Melinoe

Austin, TX

Rapper Mike Melinoe cut his teeth in the Detroit music scene before relocating to Austin. The Motor City’s loss is definitely our gain. Melinoe performed for KVRX at a Local Live taping last fall, where he demonstrated his charisma and his versatility behind the mic. Since then, he released Oo, an impressive LP that gave his oddball sensibilities plenty of space to flesh themselves out.

Ama

Austin, TX

Ama is the homegrown power pop project of Blair Robbins whose soft, syrupy voice pours out over the group’s fuzz-y tracks. They make music that makes you want to hug your friends and do other pure activities.

Indoor Creature

Austin, TX

Austin-based Indoor Creature is a favorite within the local music scene, and for good reason. They combine dreamy jazz with upbeat pop, making their music danceable and thought-provoking at the same time. The group came through the studio in July for a Local Live performance, and KVRX can’t wait to welcome them back at the fest.

Veranda

Austin, TX

Veranda lean comfortably into the lofi synth-pop genre. They combine the 2000s autotune pop vox sound with an emotional edge any lifelong emos know and love.

Alexalone

Austin, TX

Alexalone is a shoegaze artist from Austin that's been building buzz since 2018 after being featured on NPR's Austin 100 list. Songs like "learning how to play again in dadabd" are 17-minute long journeys that take the listener (all of us) on a wild ride.

Sad Cops

Denton, TX

Sad Cops just recently got done traversing the nation on their last tour, and we are counting down the days until they bring their talent down to Austin. Gaining popularity in 2016, the Denton-based group is known for their emo-rock that most definitely lives up to that description. We looking forward to getting a bit emotional during their set.

Pearl Earl

Denton, TX

Pearl Earl is a sick psychedellic group from Denton, Texas. Last December, Pearl Earl was in for Local Live and they put on an enchanting show. These amazing artists are redefining what psych-rock is.

Daphne Tunes

Austin, TX

Santiago RD, Andrew Stevens, Josh Halpern, and Peter Shults come together to provide us with mellow pop songs which are perfect for a relaxed morning or a long drive. They have a new record coming out summer of 2019, so come catch their set and maybe they’ll give us a taste of what’s to come.

Little Mazarn

Austin, TX

Little Mazarn is an Austin-based folk duo known for creating dream-like and haunting soundscapes. Lindsey Verill's vocals float over the sounds of banjo and singing saw, particularly highlighted in the equally parts heartbreaking and loving "My Love is All Around You". The duo have been guest performers on Local Live, bringing their atmospheric and minimal sounds to the KVRX studio.

Vonne

Austin, TX

Vonne is the stage name of vocalist and producer Yvonne Goodwyne, who combines elements of pop and R&B into a genre that is totally her own. Her lyrics explores love, sexuality, loss and everything in between. Sometimes upbeat, sometimes soothing, Vonne is a must-see.

Hey Cowboy!

Denton, TX

Comprised of three of the coolest girls in Texas, Hey Cowboy! makes some super fun synthpop jams. Sometimes slow, sometimes fast, every song is danceable and an experience.

Being Dead

Austin, TX

Austin's Being Dead produces lo-fi and "scary" psych-rock music. Their creed is as follows: "Death is eternal, as is rock." Let's be weird and rock out with them for eternity at KVRX Fest.

Street Sects

Austin, TX

Street Sects specialize in aggressive, noisy, power electronics with a flair for theatrics. Often shrouded in a thick cloud of fog with strobes as the only source of light, the group is known for delivering hard-hitting industrial music that's not for the faint of heart (but seriously).

Ex Romantika

Austin, TX

Ex Romantica is a 9-piece band that is known for their wonderful brass section and their fusion of funk and Latin music. Their last single "We the People" was released in November of last year, and it encapsulates the themes of freedom and empowerment often in their music. Perfect energy for KVRX Fest.

Van Mary

Austin, TX

Van Mary is one of Austin’s newer bands, but they’re already establishing themselves as a force in the local scene. Fronted by Emily Whetstone, whose powerful vocals drive the band, Van Mary is dedicated to bringing listeners garage rock that’s equal parts fun and angst. Currently working on their new album, Van Mary is a local favorite.

Chucky Blk

Austin, TX

Chucky Blk helped KVRX celebrate College Radio Day last fall, and we're thrilled to invite him back to Fest! The rapper's background as a slam poet informs his cerebral verses, but his ear for hooks makes it hard to imagine him as anything other than a musician.

Big Wy's Brass Band

Austin, TX

Comprised almost entirely of UT students, Big Wy’s Brass Band boasts a repertoire of equally-inventive covers and originals. Not only has their funky sound made them a staple at Austin house shows and local venues for years, but they have also performed in at festivals in Costa Rica and Guatemala.

Jay Wile

Austin, TX

Jay Wile is an artist and producer making pop R&B in the vein of Frank Ocean and Daniel Caesar. His laid-back, smooth tunes are the perfect charm for both home listening and a night out.

Light Horse Harry

Austin, TX

Though their roots are in Dallas, Light Horse Harry has entered the Austin music scene with their artful mix of folk, country, and rock. Self-described as "upbeat Americana," the group is able to elate and inspire listeners while maintaining an edge that distinguishes them from bands of similar ilk.

Hot Mom

Austin, TX

Tinged with irony and packed with sociopolitical disillusionment, Austin self-reporting “cute twinks and bad grrls” channel their frustrations with society into their driving punk rock. They’ll take their commentary on sexuality, politics, and pop culture to KVRX studios on Local Live, airing March 31st at 10pm on KVRX.

Fishing in Japan

Dallas, TX

Dallas-based indie pop band Fishing in Japan is the youngest act on the festival lineup, but their music is mature beyond their years. With dreamy guitar riffs and synth beats, these talented high-schoolers capture the ups and downs of young adulthood. No wonder they’re a favorite among KVRX DJs.

Nevil

Austin, TX

Nevil is the three-piece garage-punk outfit made of Emily Rose, Katelynn Garza and Tessa Castor. Although newcomers to the local scene, they’ve already made a firm impression on KVRX as the coolest band in town.

Lainey Gonzales

Austin, TX

Lainey is a singer-songwriter from UT Austin. She takes her love for indie rock, pop, and country to create songs that are easy on the ears and hard on the heart.

cement

Austin, TX

Cement... oh, Cement. Cement is the greatest Pavement cover band you've never heard of. Formed by four KVRX DJs/Malkmus superfans last fall, they are performing a very special one-time reunion set at Fest. It may be your only chance to see the artistic expression that is Cement.
